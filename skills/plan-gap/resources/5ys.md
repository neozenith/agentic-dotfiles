# The 5 Why's

- push back on the current contextual issue, performing "The 5 Why's". 
- Force critically thinking about the problem with a broader **macro** view of the project instead of hyperfocusing on the **micro* issue at hand.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                               
- Decisions MUST be made in the context of the whole project.
- Often doing this will reveal that the "problem" is actually a symptom of a deeper issue, and that the real problem is something else entirely.
- Also often doing will reveal we have already documented a decision framework and guiding values as a lens to make the decision, and that the "problem" is actually a non-issue because we have already made a decision on it.
- We often already have agentic memories about the project structure and testing structure and the reasons for why it is structured that way.
- think through unresolved issues by reflecting with "The 5 Why's" to correctly challenge the assumptions in the question at hand to make sure the decision is    
  grounded in the macro-context
